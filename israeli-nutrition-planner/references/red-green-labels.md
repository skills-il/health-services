# Israeli Front-of-Package Food Labeling (Red/Green System)

Source: Israeli Ministry of Health, Efsharibari program
Official page: https://efsharibari.health.gov.il/en/governance/legislation/unhealthy-food-labeling-law/
Regulation: Approved by Knesset Health Committee, December 2017. Implemented in two phases.

## Phase 1: January 2020 (Initial Thresholds)

| Nutrient | Per 100g (solid food) | Per 100ml (liquid food) |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Sodium | 500 mg | 400 mg |
| Total Sugars | 13.5 g | 5 g |
| Saturated Fat | 5 g | 3 g |

## Phase 2: January 2021 (Current Thresholds)

| Nutrient | Per 100g (solid food) | Per 100ml (liquid food) |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Sodium | 400 mg | 300 mg |
| Total Sugars | 10 g | 5 g |
| Saturated Fat | 4 g | 3 g |

**IMPORTANT:** Always use Phase 2 thresholds. Phase 1 is historical only.

## Red Labels (Mandatory)

- Red circle appears on the front of the package
- Contains the nutrient name (sodium, sugar, or saturated fat)
- A product can have 0, 1, 2, or 3 red labels
- Required for all packaged food products sold in Israel
- Exemption: packages with front area smaller than 25 cm2

## Green Labels (Voluntary)

- Positive marking for foods meeting health criteria
- Generally applies to:
  - Foods in their natural form
  - Minimally processed foods
  - Foods with only added spices or herbs
  - Foods with no food additives
- Based on Mediterranean diet principles with Israeli dietary adjustments
- Considers both health advantages and processing level

## Consumer Impact (2023 Survey Data)

- Over 90% of consumers report seeing red labels on packages
- Nearly 70% check for red labels when shopping
- Only 48% examine the full Nutrition Facts panel on the back

## Key Distinctions

1. Red labels mark EXCESS of specific nutrients, not overall nutritional quality
2. A product without a green label is NOT necessarily unhealthy
3. Fresh unpackaged produce (fruits, vegetables) carries no labels
4. The Israeli system is binary (red/green), unlike:
   - EU Nutri-Score (A-E scale)
   - UK traffic light system (red/amber/green per nutrient)
   - Chile/Mexico warning octagons

## Common Products by Label Status

### Typically Zero Red Labels
- Fresh produce (unpackaged)
- Plain yogurt, white cheese, labaneh
- Whole grains (bread, rice, pasta)
- Raw legumes and nuts (unsalted)
- Olive oil, tahini
- Fresh eggs, fish, poultry

### Commonly Carry Red Labels
- Sweetened breakfast cereals (sugar, sometimes sodium)
- Flavored yogurts and dairy desserts (sugar)
- Processed deli meats and sausages (sodium, saturated fat)
- Packaged snacks and chips (sodium, saturated fat)
- Sweetened beverages (sugar)
- Commercial sauces and dressings (sodium)
- Frozen prepared meals (sodium)
