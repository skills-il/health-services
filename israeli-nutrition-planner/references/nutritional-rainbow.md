# Israeli Nutritional Rainbow (Keshet Tazonit)

Source: Israeli Ministry of Health, Nutrition Department, via Efsharibari program (2018)
Official page: https://efsharibari.health.gov.il/en/eat-healthy/healthy-nutrition/the-nutritional-rainbow/nutritional-rainbow-diet/

## Overview

The Nutritional Rainbow replaced the traditional food pyramid in Israel in 2018. It organizes foods by recommended consumption frequency, emphasizing plant-based variety and Mediterranean diet principles.

## Rainbow Bands

### Green Band (Multiple Times Daily, Every Meal)

Foods: vegetables, fruits, whole grains

- Vegetables: eat a variety of colors at every meal. Raw and cooked.
- Fruits: prefer whole fruits over juice. Variety of colors.
- Whole grains: lechem maleh (whole-wheat bread), orez maleh (brown rice), bulgur, quinoa, oatmeal, whole-wheat pasta.

### Yellow Band (At Least Once Daily, From Each Sub-Group)

Three sub-groups (choose from each daily):

1. **Legumes:** lentils (adashim), chickpeas (hummus gargirim), beans (she'u'it), broad beans (ful), peas (afuna)
2. **Healthy fats from plant sources:** olive oil (shemen zayit), tahini (t'hina), nuts (egozim), avocado
3. **Unsweetened dairy or alternatives:** plain yogurt, white cheese, labaneh, unsweetened plant milks

### Orange Band (Several Times Weekly)

Foods: fish, poultry, eggs

- Vary protein sources throughout the week
- Purchase raw and prepare at home
- Prefer baking, grilling, or steaming over deep frying
- Fish recommended at least twice per week (omega-3 source)

### Pink Band (Infrequent Consumption)

Foods: red meat (beef, lamb)

- Maximum 300 grams per week of home-cooked red meat
- This limit EXCLUDES processed meats (sausages, hamburgers, pastrami, cured meats)
- Processed meats are categorized below the rainbow

### Below the Rainbow (Minimize or Avoid)

Foods: ultra-processed products

- Candy and sweets
- Sweetened beverages (sodas, fruit drinks, energy drinks)
- Processed meats (naknikiot, hotdogs, salami, pastrami)
- Packaged snacks with industrial additives and extracted food components
- These items contain high levels of sugar, sodium, saturated fat, and/or industrial additives

## Core Principles

1. Plant-based emphasis: most of the plate should be vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and legumes
2. Home cooking preferred over packaged or restaurant food
3. Season with herbs and spices (za'atar, cumin, turmeric, fresh herbs) instead of salt and sugar
4. Drink water (preferably tap water) as the primary beverage
5. Eat meals together with family when possible
6. Support environmental sustainability through food choices
7. Engage in regular physical activity alongside healthy eating

## Mediterranean Diet Alignment

The Nutritional Rainbow is explicitly grounded in Mediterranean dietary principles:
- Olive oil as primary fat source
- High legume and vegetable consumption
- Moderate fish intake
- Limited red meat
- Minimal ultra-processed foods
- Seasonal and local produce preference
