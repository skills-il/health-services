# First Aid Quick Reference with Israeli-Specific Context

A quick reference for basic first aid procedures, adapted with Israeli-specific considerations including regional hazards, climate factors, and local emergency resources.

## CPR (Hachya'a, החייאה)

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation for adults, children, and infants.

### Adult CPR (ages 12+)

| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Check responsiveness | Tap shoulders, shout "Are you okay?" (Ata beseder? / את בסדר?) |
| 2 | Call 101 (MDA) | Or ask a bystander to call while you begin CPR |
| 3 | Check breathing | Look at the chest for 5-10 seconds. No breathing or only gasping = start CPR |
| 4 | Begin chest compressions | Place heel of one hand on center of chest, other hand on top |
| 5 | Push hard and fast | Depth: 5-6 cm (2 inches). Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute |
| 6 | Give rescue breaths (if trained) | Tilt head back, lift chin, 2 breaths after every 30 compressions |
| 7 | Continue 30:2 cycle | Until MDA arrives, an AED is available, or the person begins breathing |

**Compression-only CPR**: If you are not trained in rescue breaths, continuous chest compressions without breaths is still effective and better than no CPR.

### Child CPR (ages 1-12)

| Difference from Adult | Details |
|----------------------|---------|
| Compression depth | 5 cm (about 2 inches), or one-third of chest depth |
| Hand position | Use one hand for smaller children |
| Compression-to-breath ratio | 30:2 (same as adult for single rescuer) |
| Call timing | 2 minutes of CPR before calling 101 if alone with child |

### Infant CPR (under 1 year)

| Difference from Adult | Details |
|----------------------|---------|
| Compression technique | Use 2 fingers on the lower half of the breastbone |
| Compression depth | 4 cm (about 1.5 inches) |
| Rescue breaths | Cover mouth AND nose, use gentle puffs |
| Compression-to-breath ratio | 30:2 (single rescuer) |
| Call timing | 2 minutes of CPR before calling 101 if alone |

### AED (Defibrillator, דפיברילטור)

Many public locations in Israel have AED machines. Look for the green heart-shaped sign.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Turn on the AED (press the power button) |
| 2 | Follow voice prompts (available in Hebrew and English) |
| 3 | Attach pads to bare chest as shown in the diagram on the pads |
| 4 | Make sure nobody is touching the person |
| 5 | Press "shock" button if the AED advises a shock |
| 6 | Immediately resume CPR after the shock |

**Common AED locations in Israel:**
- Shopping malls (kaniyonim)
- Train stations (tachanot rakevet)
- Sports facilities
- Large office buildings
- Synagogues (in many communities)
- Community centers (matnas)

## Severe Bleeding (Dimum Chamur, דימום חמור)

| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Call 101 | Severe bleeding is a life-threatening emergency |
| 2 | Apply direct pressure | Use a clean cloth or bandage, press firmly on the wound |
| 3 | Do not remove the cloth | If blood soaks through, add more layers on top |
| 4 | Elevate if possible | Raise the injured limb above heart level if the injury allows |
| 5 | Apply tourniquet (if trained) | For life-threatening limb bleeding only, above the wound |
| 6 | Keep the person still | Movement increases blood loss |
| 7 | Monitor for shock | Pale skin, rapid breathing, confusion, rapid pulse |

**Israeli context**: Many Israelis have first aid training from military service. If a trained bystander is present, let them take the lead. Tourniquets are more commonly available in Israel (many carry them in personal first aid kits). MDA volunteers often arrive before ambulances in populated areas.

## Burns (Kviyot, כוויות)

### By Severity

| Degree | Appearance | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| First degree (superficial) | Red, painful, no blisters | Cool water for 10-20 minutes, aloe gel, pain relief |
| Second degree (partial thickness) | Red, blisters, very painful | Cool water for 10-20 minutes, do not pop blisters, cover loosely, seek medical care |
| Third degree (full thickness) | White/charred, may be painless | Call 101 immediately, cover with clean dry cloth, do NOT apply water to large burns |

### Israeli-Specific Burn Considerations

| Situation | Context | Response |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Yom HaAtzmaut bonfire burns | Common on Independence Day celebrations | Cool water immediately, cover loosely, to ER if blisters or large area |
| Lag Ba'Omer bonfire burns | Large community bonfires, children at risk | Keep children at safe distance, cool any burn immediately |
| Solar (dud shemesh) scalding | Rooftop water heaters produce very hot water in summer | Be cautious with hot water taps, especially mid-afternoon in summer |
| Desert sun burns | Negev and Arava with extreme UV | Sunscreen, hydration, cover exposed skin. For severe sunburn with blisters, seek medical care |

### Key Burn Rules

- **DO**: Cool with clean running water for 10-20 minutes
- **DO**: Cover with a clean, non-stick bandage or cloth
- **DO NOT**: Apply butter, toothpaste, or home remedies
- **DO NOT**: Pop blisters (risk of infection)
- **DO NOT**: Remove clothing stuck to a burn
- **DO NOT**: Apply ice directly to a burn

## Choking (Chnika, חנק)

### Adult/Child Choking (Conscious)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Ask "Are you choking?" (Ata nekhnak? / את נחנקת?) |
| 2 | If they can cough, encourage forceful coughing |
| 3 | If they cannot speak, cough, or breathe: stand behind them |
| 4 | Deliver 5 back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand |
| 5 | If still choking: perform Heimlich maneuver (5 abdominal thrusts) |
| 6 | Alternate 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts until the object is expelled |
| 7 | If the person becomes unconscious, lower them to the ground and begin CPR |

### Infant Choking (Under 1 Year)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Place infant face-down on your forearm, supporting the head |
| 2 | Deliver 5 firm back blows between the shoulder blades |
| 3 | Turn infant face-up, deliver 5 chest thrusts with 2 fingers |
| 4 | Alternate back blows and chest thrusts |
| 5 | If infant becomes unresponsive, begin infant CPR |
| 6 | Call 101 |

**Do NOT** perform the Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts) on infants.

## Heatstroke (Makat Chom, מכת חום)

Heatstroke is a serious risk in Israel, especially from May through October.

### Heat-Related Conditions (Progression)

| Condition | Hebrew | Symptoms | Severity |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------|
| Heat cramps (Hitavtuyot Chom) | התכווצויות חום | Muscle cramps, sweating | Mild |
| Heat exhaustion (Hitishut Chom) | התיישות חום | Heavy sweating, weakness, nausea, fast pulse | Moderate |
| Heatstroke (Makat Chom) | מכת חום | Body temp above 40C, confusion, no sweating, seizures | Life-threatening |

### Heatstroke First Aid

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Call 101 immediately |
| 2 | Move person to shade or air-conditioned area |
| 3 | Remove excess clothing |
| 4 | Cool rapidly: apply wet cloths, fan the person, place ice packs at neck, armpits, and groin |
| 5 | If conscious, give small sips of cool water |
| 6 | Do NOT give fluids if unconscious |
| 7 | Monitor breathing and be ready for CPR |

### Israeli-Specific Heat Risks

| Location | Risk Period | Key Concerns |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Negev Desert | April - October | Extreme temperatures (40C+), limited shade, remote areas |
| Dead Sea area | Year-round (summer extreme) | Lowest point on Earth, intense heat, salt irritation |
| Jordan Valley (Bik'a) | May - September | High humidity combined with heat |
| Urban areas (Tel Aviv, Jerusalem) | June - September | Urban heat island effect, especially during sharav (heat wave) |
| Military training areas | Year-round | High physical exertion in heat, heavy gear |
| Hiking trails (shvilim) | April - October | Limited water access, sun exposure |

### Prevention in Israeli Heat

| Measure | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Hydration | Drink at least 3-4 liters of water daily in summer |
| Timing | Avoid outdoor activity 10:00-16:00 during hot months |
| Clothing | Light colors, loose-fitting, hat with brim |
| Sunscreen | SPF 30+ reapplied every 2 hours |
| Sharav awareness | During heat waves (sharav, שרב), stay indoors if possible |
| Water in vehicles | Always carry water in the car, especially in the Negev |

## Snake Bites (Neshichat Nachash, נשיכת נחש)

Israel has several venomous snake species, particularly in desert and rural areas.

### Common Venomous Snakes in Israel

| Snake | Hebrew | Location | Danger Level |
|-------|--------|----------|-------------|
| Palestine Viper (Efa) | אפעה ארצישראלית | Throughout Israel, especially rocky areas | High (most common venomous snake) |
| Desert Horned Viper | צפע חרטומים | Negev, Arava | High |
| Black Desert Cobra | נחש שחור | Negev | High |
| Carpet Viper | צפע מגוון | Arava, Jordan Valley | Moderate-High |

### Snake Bite First Aid

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Move away from the snake (do not try to catch or kill it) |
| 2 | Call 101 immediately |
| 3 | Keep the person calm and still (movement spreads venom faster) |
| 4 | Remove jewelry or tight clothing near the bite (swelling will occur) |
| 5 | Keep the bitten limb at or below heart level |
| 6 | Mark the border of any swelling with a pen and note the time |
| 7 | Transport to the nearest hospital ER |

**Do NOT:**
- Apply a tourniquet
- Cut the bite or try to suck out venom
- Apply ice directly to the bite
- Give alcohol or medications

**Important**: Israeli hospitals stock antivenom. The treating hospital may contact the Poison Control Center at Rambam (04-7771900) for guidance on antivenom administration.

## Jellyfish Stings (Akitzat Meduzah, עקיצת מדוזה)

Jellyfish are common along the Israeli Mediterranean coast, particularly during summer months (June through September).

### Common Jellyfish in Israeli Waters

| Species | Hebrew | Sting Severity | Season |
|---------|--------|---------------|--------|
| Nomad Jellyfish (Rhopilema nomadica) | מדוזת הנודד | Moderate to severe | June - September |
| Moon Jellyfish | מדוזת הירח | Mild | Year-round |
| Portuguese Man-of-War (rare) | ספינת מלחמה | Severe | Occasional |

### Jellyfish Sting First Aid

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Exit the water calmly |
| 2 | Rinse the sting area with seawater (NOT fresh water) |
| 3 | Remove visible tentacle fragments with tweezers or gloved hand |
| 4 | Apply vinegar if available (helps deactivate remaining stinging cells) |
| 5 | Apply hot water (40-45C) for 20-30 minutes, or a hot pack |
| 6 | Take over-the-counter pain relief if needed |
| 7 | Seek medical attention if difficulty breathing, chest pain, or severe reaction |

**Do NOT:**
- Rinse with fresh water (activates stinging cells)
- Rub sand on the sting area
- Urinate on the sting (this is a myth and does not help)
- Apply pressure bandage

**Beach resources**: Israeli beaches staffed by lifeguards (matzilim, מצילים) typically have vinegar and basic first aid supplies. Purple flag at the beach indicates jellyfish presence.

## Earthquake Response (Re'idat Adama, רעידת אדמה)

Israel sits on the Syrian-African Rift, making earthquakes a real risk.

### During an Earthquake

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | DROP to the ground |
| 2 | Take COVER under a sturdy table or desk |
| 3 | HOLD ON until the shaking stops |
| 4 | If outdoors, move away from buildings, power lines, and trees |
| 5 | If driving, pull over, stay in the vehicle |

### After an Earthquake

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Check yourself and others for injuries |
| 2 | If trapped, tap on pipes or walls (do not shout, to conserve air) |
| 3 | If the building is damaged, evacuate carefully |
| 4 | Do not use elevators |
| 5 | Listen to Home Front Command (Pikud HaOref, פיקוד העורף) instructions |
| 6 | Be prepared for aftershocks |

### Israeli-Specific Earthquake Context

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Risk zones | Jordan Valley, Dead Sea area, and northern Israel are highest risk |
| Building codes | Post-1980 buildings are built to seismic standards; older buildings may not be |
| TAMA 38 | National plan to reinforce older buildings (tochni'it metaar artzit) |
| Home Front Command | Pikud HaOref provides earthquake preparedness information and alerts |
| Emergency kit | Water, flashlight, battery-powered radio, first aid kit, copies of ID documents |

## General First Aid Kit Contents

Every household in Israel should have a first aid kit containing:

| Item | Hebrew | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| Adhesive bandages (various sizes) | פלסטרים | Minor cuts and scrapes |
| Sterile gauze pads | פדי גזה סטריליים | Wound coverage |
| Elastic bandage | תחבושת אלסטית | Sprains, pressure |
| Triangular bandage | תחבושת משולשת | Sling, large wound coverage |
| Medical tape | פלסטר רפואי | Securing bandages |
| Antiseptic wipes | מגבונים חיטויים | Wound cleaning |
| Tweezers | פינצטה | Splinter or tentacle removal |
| Scissors | מספריים | Cutting bandages |
| Instant cold pack | שקית קרח מיידי | Swelling, heat exposure |
| CPR face shield | מגן פנים להחייאה | Barrier for rescue breaths |
| Pain reliever (acetaminophen/ibuprofen) | משכך כאבים | Pain and fever |
| Tourniquet | חסם עורקים | Severe limb bleeding (if trained) |
| Emergency blanket | שמיכת חירום | Shock, cold exposure |
| Flashlight | פנס | Power outages, emergencies |
