# Guide to Therapy Types Available in Israel

## Overview

Israel offers a wide range of evidence-based psychological treatments through both the public health system (kupot cholim) and private practice. Understanding the differences between therapy types helps patients make informed choices about their care.

## Detailed Guide by Therapy Type

### CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, טיפול קוגניטיבי-התנהגותי)

**What it is:** A structured, goal-oriented therapy that focuses on identifying and changing unhelpful thought patterns and behaviors. The therapist and patient work together on specific problems with concrete strategies.

**How it works in practice:**
- Sessions are typically 50 minutes, once a week
- The therapist assigns "homework" between sessions (thought diaries, behavioral experiments)
- Focus is on current problems, not deep exploration of childhood
- Progress is measured with standardized questionnaires

**Most effective for:**
- Generalized anxiety disorder (hafaraat charda kolelet)
- Panic disorder (hafaraat panika)
- Depression (dichon)
- OCD (hafaraat obsesivit-compulsivit)
- Social anxiety (charda chevratit)
- Specific phobias (fobiot)
- Insomnia (nedudei sheina)

**Availability through kupot cholim:**
- Available at all four kupot cholim
- CBT is the most commonly offered therapy type in the public system
- Wait time: typically 2-6 weeks
- Number of sessions: usually 12-16 per treatment course

**Finding a CBT therapist:**
- Through your kupat cholim (free/subsidized)
- Israeli CBT Association referral directory
- "Terapist" website (terapist.co.il) with CBT filter
- University training clinics (reduced rates)

### EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)

**What it is:** A trauma-focused therapy that uses guided eye movements (or other bilateral stimulation) to help the brain process and integrate traumatic memories. It does not require the patient to describe the trauma in detail.

**How it works in practice:**
- Sessions are 60-90 minutes
- The therapist asks the patient to recall a traumatic memory while following a moving visual stimulus
- The brain's natural processing mechanisms are activated, reducing the emotional charge of the memory
- Patients often report significant relief within 3-6 sessions

**Most effective for:**
- PTSD (hafaraat stress post-traumatit)
- Acute trauma response
- Childhood trauma
- Complex grief
- Performance anxiety
- Phobias with a traumatic origin

**Post-October 7 expansion:**
- All four kupot cholim significantly expanded EMDR services
- Thousands of therapists received accelerated EMDR training
- EMDR availability is now the highest it has ever been in Israel
- Priority access for directly affected individuals

**Availability through kupot cholim:**
- Available at all four kupot cholim (expanded significantly)
- Wait time: 2-8 weeks depending on demand
- Usually 8-12 sessions covered

### Psychodynamic Therapy (טיפול פסיכודינמי)

**What it is:** A longer-term therapy rooted in psychoanalytic theory. It explores unconscious patterns, early relationships, and how past experiences shape current behavior. The therapeutic relationship itself is a tool for understanding interpersonal patterns.

**How it works in practice:**
- Sessions are 50 minutes, typically 1-2 times per week
- Open-ended exploration rather than structured techniques
- Focus on understanding "why" rather than "what to do"
- The relationship with the therapist reveals patterns seen in other relationships
- Dream analysis and free association may be used

**Most effective for:**
- Recurring relationship problems
- Personality disorders
- Chronic low-grade depression (dysthymia)
- Difficulty understanding one's own emotions and motivations
- Identity issues
- Long-standing patterns that have not responded to shorter therapies

**Availability through kupot cholim:**
- Limited availability; kupot cholim tend to favor shorter-term therapies
- Some kupot offer psychodynamic therapy for cases where CBT has not been effective
- Most psychodynamic therapy in Israel occurs in private practice

### DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy, טיפול דיאלקטי-התנהגותי)

**What it is:** A specialized therapy originally developed for borderline personality disorder. It combines CBT techniques with mindfulness and acceptance strategies. Treatment typically involves both individual sessions and a skills group.

**How it works in practice:**
- Individual therapy sessions (weekly, 50 minutes)
- Skills group (weekly, 2 hours) covering: mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness
- Phone coaching between sessions for crisis situations
- Homework and diary cards to track emotions and skills use

**Most effective for:**
- Borderline personality disorder (hafaraat ishiut gvulit)
- Self-harm behaviors
- Suicidal ideation (chronic)
- Severe emotion dysregulation
- Binge eating disorder
- Substance use disorders

**Availability through kupot cholim:**
- Some kupot offer DBT programs (especially Clalit and Maccabi)
- Usually requires a specific referral from a psychiatrist
- Private DBT programs available at specialized clinics

### Group Therapy (טיפול קבוצתי)

**What it is:** Therapy conducted in a group setting (typically 6-12 participants) facilitated by one or two therapists. Participants share experiences, provide mutual support, and learn from each other.

**Types of groups available in Israel:**
- General support groups (depression, anxiety)
- Trauma processing groups (expanded after October 7)
- Grief and bereavement groups
- Addiction recovery groups (12-step and alternatives)
- Social skills groups
- Anger management groups
- Groups for specific populations (new parents, cancer patients, bereaved families)

**Availability through kupot cholim:**
- Available at all kupot cholim
- Often shorter wait times than individual therapy
- Usually free or minimal copay
- Groups run for a set period (typically 12-20 sessions)

### Family Therapy (טיפול משפחתי)

**What it is:** Therapy that treats the family as a system rather than focusing on one individual. A family therapist works with multiple family members together to improve communication, resolve conflicts, and address behavioral problems.

**Most effective for:**
- Parent-child conflicts
- Child behavioral problems
- Adolescent issues (school refusal, eating disorders, substance use)
- Families coping with illness or loss
- Blended family adjustment
- Divorce-related family issues

**Availability:**
- Available through some kupat cholim programs (especially for children)
- Tipat Chalav (well-baby clinics) may refer for family therapy
- Private practice: 400-800 NIS per session (sessions are typically longer, 60-90 minutes)

### Art Therapy (טיפול באומנות)

**What it is:** A form of therapy that uses creative expression (painting, drawing, sculpting, collage) as a way to explore emotions, process trauma, and develop insight. The focus is on the process of creating, not artistic skill.

**Most effective for:**
- Children with emotional or behavioral difficulties
- Trauma survivors (especially those who struggle to verbalize)
- Elderly patients with dementia
- People with developmental disabilities
- Grief and loss processing

**Availability:**
- Some kupot cholim offer art therapy, especially for children
- Available at mental health centers and child development institutes
- Private: 250-500 NIS per session
- Israel has a strong art therapy training tradition (academic programs at Haifa University and Lesley University)

### Play Therapy (טיפול במשחק)

**What it is:** A therapy modality for children (typically ages 3-12) that uses play as the primary means of communication and expression. The therapist creates a safe play environment and uses the child's play to understand and address emotional issues.

**Most effective for:**
- Children with anxiety or fears
- Behavioral problems at home or school
- Adjustment difficulties (divorce, new sibling, relocation)
- Trauma in young children
- Selective mutism
- Social difficulties

**Availability through kupot cholim:**
- Available through child development services (sherut hitpatchut hayeled)
- Referral through family doctor or pediatrician
- Tipat Chalav may also refer

## How to Choose the Right Therapy

| If You Are Dealing With... | Consider Starting With |
|---------------------------|----------------------|
| Anxiety or panic attacks | CBT (most evidence-based for anxiety) |
| PTSD or trauma flashbacks | EMDR (faster results for trauma) or trauma-focused CBT |
| Depression (first episode) | CBT or medication (or both) |
| Depression (chronic/recurring) | Psychodynamic therapy, possibly combined with medication |
| Self-harm or emotional instability | DBT (specifically designed for these issues) |
| Relationship problems (patterns) | Psychodynamic therapy |
| Couple conflict | Couples therapy with a licensed family therapist |
| Child behavioral issues | Play therapy (ages 3-12) or family therapy |
| Social isolation or loneliness | Group therapy |
| Not sure what I need | Start with your kupat cholim intake; they will assess and recommend |

## Who Can Provide Therapy in Israel

| Title | Hebrew | Training | Can Prescribe Medication | Typical Cost |
|-------|--------|----------|------------------------|-------------|
| Psychiatrist (Psikh'iater) | פסיכיאטר | MD + psychiatric residency | Yes | 500-900 NIS |
| Clinical Psychologist (Psikholog Klini) | פסיכולוג קליני | MA/PhD + clinical internship | No | 300-600 NIS |
| Clinical Social Worker (Oveid Sotsiali Klini) | עובד סוציאלי קליני | MSW + clinical specialization | No | 200-450 NIS |
| Art Therapist (Metapel B'Omanut) | מטפל באומנות | MA in art therapy | No | 250-500 NIS |
| Counseling Psychologist | פסיכולוג ייעוצי | MA + counseling internship | No | 250-500 NIS |

**Important:** Only a psychiatrist can prescribe psychiatric medication. Psychologists and social workers can provide therapy but not medication. If both therapy and medication are needed, the patient may see two different professionals.

## Getting the Most from Your Kupat Cholim

1. **Know your rights.** Under the 2015 reform, mental health care is covered for all residents. You do not need a "serious" condition to qualify.

2. **Ask about therapy type.** When contacting your kupat cholim, ask specifically for the therapy type that matches your needs (e.g., "I want CBT for anxiety" or "I need EMDR for trauma").

3. **Request a different therapist if needed.** If the assigned therapist is not a good fit after 2-3 sessions, you have the right to request reassignment.

4. **Check SHABAN benefits.** Supplementary insurance may provide additional sessions, faster access, or coverage for private therapists.

5. **Combine approaches.** It is common and effective to combine therapy with medication, especially for moderate-to-severe depression and anxiety.

6. **Explore external therapist reimbursement.** If wait times are too long, some kupot will partially reimburse sessions with approved private therapists (shlichut, שליחות).
