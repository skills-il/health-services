# Israeli Mental Health Crisis Hotlines Directory

## Overview

Israel has an extensive network of crisis hotlines and emotional support services. These lines are staffed by trained volunteers and professionals who provide immediate support in multiple languages. This directory organizes all available services by type and population served.

## Emergency Numbers

If someone is in immediate physical danger or threatening to harm themselves or others:

| Service | Number | When to Call |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Magen David Adom (MDA, ambulance) | 101 | Medical emergency, person has harmed themselves |
| Police | 100 | Immediate danger to self or others, violent situation |
| Fire and Rescue | 102 | Fire or entrapment |
| General Emergency | 112 | Any emergency (connects to appropriate service) |

## Tips for Calling Crisis Lines

1. **You do not need to be in a "real" crisis.** These lines exist for anyone experiencing emotional difficulty, not just emergencies. Feeling lonely, anxious, or overwhelmed all qualify.

2. **Calls are anonymous.** You do not need to identify yourself. No information is recorded or shared with anyone.

3. **If the line is busy, try again or try another service.** Peak times (late evening, holidays) may have wait times. Sahar (online chat) can be a good alternative when phone lines are busy.

4. **You can call on behalf of someone else.** If you are worried about a friend or family member, you can call for guidance on how to help them.

5. **Language is not a barrier.** ERAN and Natal offer services in multiple languages. If the first responder does not speak your language, ask to be transferred.

6. **These services are free.** No charge for any crisis line call in Israel.
