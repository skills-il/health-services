# Detailed IVF Process in Israel: Step by Step

## Overview

Israel's IVF (in vitro fertilization, הפריה חוץ גופית) system is one of the most accessible in the world. The national health basket covers unlimited IVF cycles until two live births, for women up to age 45. This guide walks through every stage of the process, from initial consultation through pregnancy test.

## Stage 1: Getting a Referral

| Step | Details | Timeline |
|------|---------|----------|
| Visit your gynecologist (rofe nashim, רופא נשים) | Describe how long you have been trying to conceive (typically 12 months, or 6 months if over 35) | Day 1 |
| Obtain a referral (hafnaya, הפניה) | Your gynecologist writes a referral to a fertility specialist (rofe poryut, רופא פוריות) | Same visit |
| Choose a fertility clinic (yechida leporyut, יחידה לפוריות) | Select from your kupat cholim's approved list of fertility units | 1-2 weeks |

**What kupat cholim covers at this stage:** Gynecologist visit, referral, all diagnostic tests ordered.

**Fertility clinics in major hospitals:**

|----------|------|-------|

## Stage 2: Initial Consultation and Diagnostics

The fertility specialist conducts a thorough evaluation of both partners.

### Tests for the Woman

| Test | Hebrew Name | Purpose | When in Cycle |
|------|-------------|---------|--------------|
| Hormonal blood panel | פאנל הורמונלי | FSH, LH, estradiol, AMH, prolactin, TSH | Day 2-3 of menstrual cycle |
| AMH (Anti-Mullerian Hormone) | AMH | Indicates ovarian reserve (egg supply) | Any day |
| Transvaginal ultrasound | אולטרסאונד טרנסווגינלי | Counts antral follicles, checks uterine structure | Day 2-3 of cycle |
| HSG (Hysterosalpingography) | בדיקת HSG / צילום רחם וחצוצרות | Checks if fallopian tubes are open | Day 7-11 of cycle |
| Infectious disease screening | בדיקות זיהומים | HIV, Hepatitis B/C, syphilis, rubella immunity | Any time |
| Genetic carrier screening | בדיקות נשאות גנטיות | Screens for common genetic conditions | Any time |

### Tests for the Man

| Test | Hebrew Name | Purpose |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Semen analysis (bdikat zera, בדיקת זרע) | ספירת זרע | Sperm count, motility, morphology |
| Infectious disease screening | בדיקות זיהומים | HIV, Hepatitis B/C |
| Hormonal panel (if indicated) | פאנל הורמונלי | Testosterone, FSH (only if semen analysis abnormal) |

**What kupat cholim covers:** All diagnostic tests listed above are fully covered.

**Timeline:** Diagnostics typically take 2-4 weeks to complete, depending on cycle timing.

## Stage 3: Treatment Plan

After reviewing all test results, the fertility specialist creates a personalized treatment protocol.

| Protocol Type | Description | Typical Use Case |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Long protocol (protokol aroch) | Start GnRH agonist in previous cycle to suppress hormones, then stimulate | Standard for most patients |
| Short/Antagonist protocol (protokol katsar) | Start stimulation immediately, add antagonist mid-cycle to prevent premature ovulation | Patients with low ovarian reserve, older patients |
| Mini/Mild stimulation | Lower medication doses, fewer eggs retrieved | Patients who over-respond, or preference for gentler approach |
| Natural cycle IVF | No stimulation, retrieve the single naturally selected egg | Specific medical indications |

**What kupat cholim covers:** Consultation and treatment plan development, all medications prescribed.

## Stage 4: Hormonal Stimulation (Girui Shachlotot, גירוי שחלות)

| Day | What Happens | Details |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| Days 1-2 | Begin injections | Self-administered subcutaneous injections (pen or syringe) |
| Days 3-5 | Monitoring begins | Blood tests and ultrasound every 2-3 days |
| Days 6-8 | Dose adjustment | Doctor may adjust medication dose based on response |
| Days 9-11 | Follicle growth monitoring | Ultrasound to measure follicle sizes |
| Day 10-14 | Trigger shot | When follicles reach 17-20mm, trigger injection (Ovitrelle or similar) |

**Common medications used:**

| Medication | Purpose | Administration |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Gonal-F / Puregon | FSH, stimulates follicle growth | Daily subcutaneous injection |
| Menopur | FSH + LH, stimulates follicles | Daily subcutaneous injection |
| Cetrotide / Orgalutran | GnRH antagonist, prevents premature ovulation | Daily subcutaneous injection (mid-cycle) |
| Ovitrelle / Pregnyl | hCG trigger, finalizes egg maturation | Single injection, 36 hours before retrieval |
| Decapeptyl | GnRH agonist (long protocol) or alternative trigger | Varies by protocol |

**What kupat cholim covers:** All hormonal medications, all monitoring visits (blood tests and ultrasounds).

**Practical tips:**
- Injections are self-administered at home; the fertility nurse teaches you how
- Store medications in the refrigerator (most require refrigeration)
- Take injections at approximately the same time each day
- Monitoring visits are usually early morning (7:00-8:00) at the fertility clinic
- You may feel bloated and uncomfortable during stimulation; this is normal
- Avoid strenuous exercise during stimulation

## Stage 5: Egg Retrieval (Aspiratzia, אספירציה / Shivat Bitziot, שאיבת ביציות)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Timing | 36 hours after trigger shot |
| Duration | 15-30 minutes |
| Anesthesia | Light sedation (hargaa, הרגעה) or local anesthesia |
| Procedure | Ultrasound-guided needle through vaginal wall to aspirate follicles |
| Recovery | Rest for 1-2 hours in recovery room, go home same day |
| After retrieval | Mild cramping normal, avoid driving that day, rest recommended |
| Sick leave | Entitled to 1 day of sick leave (ishur machala) |

**What kupat cholim covers:** The entire retrieval procedure, anesthesia, facility fees.

**Expected number of eggs by age (rough averages):**

| Age | Typical Eggs Retrieved | Notes |
|-----|----------------------|-------|
| Under 30 | 10-20 | High response expected |
| 30-35 | 8-15 | Good response typical |
| 35-38 | 6-12 | Gradually declining |
| 38-40 | 4-8 | Response may vary significantly |
| 40-42 | 2-6 | Lower numbers expected |
| 42-45 | 1-4 | Each egg is valuable |

## Stage 6: Fertilization and Embryo Development

| Day | Event | Details |
|-----|-------|---------|
| Day 0 | Egg retrieval + insemination | Eggs are fertilized with sperm (standard IVF or ICSI) |
| Day 1 | Fertilization check | Embryologist checks how many eggs fertilized successfully |
| Day 2 | Cell division | Embryos should be at 2-4 cell stage |
| Day 3 | Continued growth | Embryos at 6-8 cell stage; day-3 transfers happen here if applicable |
| Day 5 | Blastocyst stage | Embryos that reach blastocyst are optimal for transfer; day-5 transfer is most common |
| Day 5-6 | Freezing | Extra quality embryos are frozen (vitrification) for future use |

**IVF vs. ICSI:**

| Method | When Used | How It Works |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Standard IVF | Normal sperm parameters | Eggs and sperm placed together in dish; sperm penetrate naturally |
| ICSI | Low sperm count/motility, previous fertilization failure | Single sperm injected directly into each egg |

**What kupat cholim covers:** All laboratory work, embryology, ICSI if needed, embryo freezing.

## Stage 7: Embryo Transfer (Hachzarat Ubarim, החזרת עוברים)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Timing | Day 3 or Day 5 after retrieval (doctor decides) |
| Duration | 5-10 minutes |
| Anesthesia | None required (painless procedure) |
| Procedure | Thin catheter guided through cervix, embryo(s) placed in uterus |
| Number transferred | 1-2 embryos (single embryo transfer increasingly preferred to reduce twin risk) |
| After transfer | Rest for 15-30 minutes, then go home |
| Activity restrictions | Avoid strenuous activity for 2-3 days; otherwise normal activity is fine |

**What kupat cholim covers:** Transfer procedure, all associated costs.

## Stage 8: The Two-Week Wait (Hamtana, המתנה)

| Day Post-Transfer | What Happens |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Days 1-3 | Implantation window (embryo attaches to uterine lining) |
| Days 4-7 | Continue progesterone support (vaginal suppositories or injections) |
| Days 7-10 | hCG hormone begins rising if implantation occurred |
| Day 12-14 | Blood test for beta-hCG at fertility clinic |

**Medications during the two-week wait:**

| Medication | Purpose | Administration |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Progesterone (Utrogestan, Endometrin, Crinone) | Supports uterine lining for implantation | Vaginal suppositories, 2-3 times daily |
| Estrogen (Estrofem) | Sometimes prescribed for additional lining support | Oral or vaginal |
| Aspirin (low dose) | Sometimes prescribed to support blood flow to uterus | Oral |

**What kupat cholim covers:** All post-transfer medications, the pregnancy blood test.

## Stage 9: Results

| Result | Next Steps |
|--------|-----------|
| Positive beta-hCG | Continue progesterone; follow-up blood test in 2 days to confirm doubling; ultrasound at 6-7 weeks |
| Negative beta-hCG | Stop progesterone; schedule follow-up with fertility doctor to discuss next steps |
| Low/slow-rising beta-hCG | Additional monitoring; may indicate ectopic pregnancy or early miscarriage |

## Frozen Embryo Transfer (FET, החזרת עובר מוקפא)

If extra embryos were frozen, they can be used in future cycles without repeating egg retrieval.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Preparation | Hormone protocol to prepare the uterine lining (typically 2-3 weeks) |
| Procedure | Same as fresh transfer (painless, 5-10 minutes) |
| Success rates | Comparable to fresh transfers; sometimes higher due to better uterine preparation |
| Cost | Fully covered by health basket (no additional egg retrieval needed) |
| Storage | Embryos can be stored for years; annual storage fee applies |

## Typical Complete IVF Timeline

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Referral to first consultation | 2-6 weeks |
| Diagnostics | 2-4 weeks |
| Treatment plan | 1-2 weeks |
