# Cancer-Type-Equitable Rights Reference

Most of the rights in this skill apply to **all cancer types**, but skill-level documentation has historically biased toward breast cancer (because One in Nine is well-organized and frequently cited). This file documents the rights that every cancer type can claim, with cancer-type-specific details where they matter.

## 1. Co-pay exemptions for severe illness (all cancer types)

Cancer is classified as a "severe illness" (מחלות קשות). Patients are exempt from co-payment for:

- Specialist physician visits (including psychiatry)
- Outpatient clinic visits at hospitals
- Emergency room when accompanied by relevant medical documents
- Imaging: X-ray, ultrasound, nuclear medicine, CT, echocardiography, MRI
- Diagnostic tests: EEG, EMG, audiometry, ergometry
- Gastroenterology and sleep disorder clinics
- Dietitian and podiatrist consultations
- Medications (within the basket)

**Activation:** the HMO should apply the exemption automatically based on the oncology diagnosis code. If it does not appear in the system, request activation from the HMO with a specialist letter confirming the cancer diagnosis.

**Source:** Kolzchut, "פטור מתשלום עבור תרופות ושירותים רפואיים לחולים במחלות קשות".

## 2. Dental treatment funding

The HMO funds dental treatment for cancer patients with tier-specific rules:

| Stage | HMO coverage of basket dental treatments |
|-------|------------------------------------------|
| Before chemo/radiation begins | 50% of basket treatments at HMO tariff |
| After chemo/radiation ends (within 24 months of completion) | 100% of basket treatments at HMO tariff |
| Head/neck radiation patients | 100% with **no time limit** (2013 basket update) |
| Jaw resection (post-tumor) | 100% with no time limit, including extended rehab (partial dentures, crowns, implants) |

**Critical for head/neck radiation patients:** start dental rehab BEFORE radiation begins. Radiation causes salivary gland damage, leading to "radiation caries" and risk of osteoradionecrosis (jaw bone death) if untreated cavities are present at time of treatment. Pre-radiation extractions and restorations are part of the standard of care.

**Critical for chemo patients:** severe oral mucositis and immunosuppression mean any active dental infection is dangerous. Pre-chemo dental clearance (cleaning, addressing active caries) is recommended.

**Activation:** contact the HMO dental department with an oncology referral letter.

**Sources:** Kolzchut "מימון טיפולי שיניים לחולי סרטן"; Clalit oncology dental page; Maccabi oncology eligibility page.

## 3. Surgery wait-time guarantee + Form 17

Cancer patients requiring certain surgeries are entitled to have surgery within a defined maximum wait period from the decision-to-operate date.

**If the HMO cannot meet the timeline:**
1. Patient contacts the HMO's **Arrangements Center (מוקד הסדרים)** — each HMO operates one
2. The Center has **24 hours** to find an earlier date at any covered facility
3. If still unsuccessful, the Center must issue **Form 17 (התחייבות)** — a commitment letter the patient can present at any authorized facility allowed to perform the procedure, at no additional cost

**Practical use:** the Form 17 mechanism is most often invoked for time-sensitive cancer surgeries where the home HMO's hospital has a backlog. It enables surgery at a competitor HMO's facility or a private hospital with the home HMO paying.

**Source:** Kolzchut, "חולי סרטן" (EN page).

## 4. Hereditary cancer (BRCA, Lynch, Li-Fraumeni, FAP) — all syndromes covered

The basket covers genetic testing for cancer patients diagnosed with cancer **and** for first-degree relatives of known carriers. This applies across the full range of recognized hereditary cancer syndromes:

| Syndrome | Cancers screened |
|----------|------------------|
| BRCA1 / BRCA2 | Breast, ovarian, prostate, pancreatic |
| Lynch syndrome / HNPCC | Colorectal, endometrial, ovarian, gastric, urothelial |
| Li-Fraumeni | Sarcoma, CNS, adrenal, breast, leukemia |
| FAP (familial adenomatous polyposis) | Colorectal (and duodenal, thyroid, hepatoblastoma) |
| MEN1 / MEN2 | Endocrine (parathyroid, pancreas, pituitary, thyroid) |
| von Hippel-Lindau | Renal, hemangioblastoma, pheochromocytoma |
| PTEN (Cowden) | Breast, thyroid, endometrial |

Positive carriers are entitled to **structured high-risk surveillance**:
- BRCA: annual breast MRI + mammography from age 25, transvaginal US + CA-125 every 6 months from age 30
- Lynch: colonoscopy every 1-2 years from age 20-25
- FAP: colonoscopy/sigmoidoscopy from age 10-12

**For non-breast hereditary cancers,** Bracha (עמותת ברכה, Guidestar 580508075) provides peer support — though their main focus is BRCA. For other syndromes, surveillance programs are run through the genetic clinics at major hospitals (Sheba, Hadassah, Ichilov, Rambam).

**Source:** Kolzchut "בדיקות גנטיות"; ICA BRCA section (https://www.cancer.org.il/subcategories/brca/).

## 5. Home chemotherapy

Certain regimens can be administered at the patient's home, basket-covered. Two delivery modes:

- **HMO home-treatment service:** specialized oncology nurse visits the home to administer chemo via an existing port. All infusion supplies provided.
- **Portable infuser pump:** continuous-infusion drugs (e.g., 5-FU as part of FOLFOX/FOLFIRI for colorectal) via a small wearable pump connected to a central line. Patient returns to hospital only when the pump empties.

**Best candidates:**
- Patients with severe mobility limitations
- Parents of young children who cannot easily travel for treatment
- Immunocompromised patients with high infection risk in hospital settings
- Patients living far from the treating center

Eligibility for the home option is regimen-specific. Ask the treating oncologist or oncology nurse coordinator.

**Source:** Kolzchut Rights Guide for Chemotherapy Patients; Israeli Cancer Association.

## 6. Medical cannabis (post-April 2024 reform — prescription only)

As of 1 April 2024, the medical cannabis pathway changed. **Cancer patients no longer need a license** — only a **prescription from a certified physician**.

**Eligibility:**
- Patients in active oncological treatment (chemotherapy, immunotherapy, radiation)
- Patients with treatment-related pain, nausea/vomiting, appetite loss, or sleep disturbance
- Patients with cancer-related chronic pain after treatment ends

**Process:**
1. Patient sees a physician certified to prescribe medical cannabis (most oncologists are; the HMO can refer)
2. If approved, the physician issues a prescription in the same visit (no MOH license process for cancer patients post-reform)
3. Patient buys at any certified pharmacy

**Costs:** annual prescription cost ~₪186. The older license-based process (~₪372/year) still applies to non-cancer indications and edge cases.

**Source:** gov.il + ICA (cancer.org.il/articles/13264) + Kolzchut.

## 7. Post-surgical rehabilitation by cancer site

The basket and supplementary tiers cover a wide range of post-cancer rehabilitation. Specific entitlements vary by surgical site:

| Cancer site | Rehabilitation rights |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Breast | Mastectomy reconstruction (basic basket, immediate or delayed). External breast prosthesis and specialized bras. Lymphedema compression garments and therapy. |
| Colorectal / GI | Stoma supplies (colostomy/ileostomy bags, barriers, accessories) -- ongoing entitlement. Continuity-restoration surgery when medically feasible. Pelvic floor rehab post-LAR. |
| Head and neck | Dental rehabilitation post-radiation (see section 2 above). Voice rehabilitation and speech therapy. Swallowing rehabilitation. |
| Limb sarcoma / bone | Prosthetic limbs and orthotic devices. Mobility aids (wheelchairs, walkers). |
| Laryngeal | Voice prosthesis (tracheoesophageal puncture). Electrolarynx device. Speech therapy. |
| Prostate / bladder | Continence supplies (pads, catheters). Pelvic floor rehabilitation. |
| Brain / CNS | Cognitive rehabilitation. Occupational therapy. Mobility aids if applicable. |
| Any cancer type (general) | HMO-funded wig coverage for chemo-related hair loss. Equipment loans via ICA, Yad Sarah (100+ branches), Ezer Mizion. |

**Activation:** contact the hospital rehabilitation department and the HMO rehabilitation coordinator (רכז שיקום).

**Source:** Kolzchut "אביזרי ומכשירי שיקום".

## 8. Family sick days for cancer caregiving

Cancer caregivers have substantial entitlements that are routinely missed:

| Caregiver | Days per year | Source |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Spouse caring for cancer patient | Up to 60 (from caregiver's accrued balance) | Kolzchut "ימי מחלה בגין טיפול בבן משפחה" |
| Parent of a child under 18 with cancer | Up to 90 | Kolzchut "זכותון מטופלים בכימותרפיה" |
| Single parent (sole caregiver missing work) | Up to 110 | Kolzchut chemo rights guide |

These do not require employer pre-approval beyond standard sick-leave procedures. Days are deducted from the caregiver's accrued sick balance, then from accrued vacation if exhausted.

## 9. Arnona discount

40% municipal property tax discount for cancer patients with either (a) general disability pension at 75%+ earning-capacity loss, or (b) 90%+ medical disability. Applied automatically by the local authority via NII lists, but verify with the municipality (especially if the property is not registered in the patient's name).

**Source:** Kolzchut "הנחה בארנונה לחולי סרטן".

## 10. Volunteer transport

The Israeli Cancer Association runs a national volunteer-driver program for cancer patients traveling to chemo/radiation appointments. Contact: oncology social worker or ICA Telemeda (1-800-599-995).

**NOT:** Road to Recovery (theroadtorecovery.org.il) — that organization's mission is transporting Palestinian patients from West Bank/Gaza checkpoints to Israeli hospitals, NOT a generic Israeli cancer-patient driver pool.

## 12. Cancer-type-specific worked examples

### Example: Colorectal cancer with stoma
1. Hospital oncology social worker first
2. Step 3a (general disability) + Step 3b (שר"מ paper track during active chemo/radiation; reassess at 6 months for 112% if stoma/neuropathy/fatigue justify)
3. Stoma supplies (bags, barriers, accessories) -- ongoing basket entitlement via HMO rehab coordinator
4. Co-pay exemption for follow-up clinic visits and imaging
5. Section 9(5) tax exemption if eligibility threshold met
6. 40% arnona discount once disability percentage is established
7. Spouse: up to 60 sick days/year for caregiving

### Example: Pediatric leukemia
1. Pediatric oncology social worker first
2. Disabled-child allowance at 235% = ₪9,126/month during active chemo/radiation
3. Fertility preservation BEFORE treatment for post-pubertal adolescents (sperm/egg banking); ovarian/testicular tissue freezing for pre-pubertal
4. Parent: 90 sick days/year (110 if single parent / sole caregiver)
5. NGOs: Larger Than Life, Zichron Menachem, Ezer Mizion (bone marrow), Halil HaOr (leukemia/lymphoma family resources)
6. Co-pay exemption applies to all child's visits and tests
7. Out-of-town families: Ezer Mizion's Oranit House

### Example: Head and neck cancer starting radiation
1. Hospital oncology social worker first
2. Dental treatment funding BEFORE radiation begins (CRITICAL — head/neck patients have no time limit per 2013 basket update; pre-radiation dental work prevents osteoradionecrosis)
3. General disability + שר"מ paper track; head/neck radiation often justifies a 112% committee request (mucositis prevents eating, neck fibrosis affects mobility, profound fatigue)
4. Voice + swallowing therapy basket-covered post-radiation
5. Medical cannabis for radiation pain and mucositis-related appetite/sleep issues -- prescription only post-April 2024
6. Co-pay exemption for specialist follow-ups, imaging, dietitian (often essential during radiation when eating is impaired)
7. ICA Rights Center (helpdesk 03-5721678) helps build the dental funding case if initially refused

### Example: Hereditary cancer (BRCA carrier without diagnosis)
1. Genetic counselor referral via family doctor or oncology
2. Genetic testing covered under basket for first-degree relatives of known carriers
3. If positive: structured surveillance program (annual breast MRI + mammography from 25, transvaginal US + CA-125 from 30 for BRCA)
4. Bracha + ICA BRCA section for peer support
5. Risk-reducing options (prophylactic mastectomy, oophorectomy) covered by basket with appropriate clinical indication
