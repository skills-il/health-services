# Experimental, Off-Label, and Unregistered-Drug Pathways for Israeli Cancer Patients

> Disclaimer: bureaucracy reference, not legal or medical advice. Pathway names, regulation numbers, and supplementary-insurance figures are 2025/2026 values. Verify against the cited source URL before acting on any specific number.

## The five pathways at a glance

| Pathway | What it is | Who pays | Legal basis |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Health basket | Drug is in the 2026 basket for the indication | HMO basic basket | Sal HaBriut |
| Exceptions committee (ועדת חריגים) | HMO funds an out-of-basket drug case-by-case | HMO budget | Internal HMO procedure |
| Form 29c (תקנה 29ג') | Permit to import an unregistered drug for one patient | Patient or another payer (separate) | Pharmacists Regulations (Preparations), 1986 §29 |
| Compassionate use (טיפול חמלה) | Pharma company supplies an unregistered/experimental drug free under MOH approval | Pharmaceutical company sponsor | MOH circular (~2006) |
| Clinical trial (ניסוי קליני) | Patient enrolled in a structured trial | Trial sponsor | People's Health Regulations (Medical Research on Human Subjects), 1980 |

## 1. Form 29c — individual import of an unregistered drug

**Legal basis**: תקנה 29 לתקנות הרוקחים (תכשירים), התשמ"ו-1986. The patient-level application is **טופס 29ג'**.

**Who files**: the **treating physician** files via the MOH **Pharmacy Division (אגף הרוקחות)**. The 29c is also the legal basis for an Israeli licensed pharmacy to import the drug.

**Requirements**:
- Medical justification by the physician
- Drug is registered in a recognized country (FDA, EMA, etc.) for the same indication
- No registered Israeli alternative

**Validity**: 6 months. Renewable.

**CRITICAL — what 29c is NOT**: 29c is an **import permit only**, not a funding source. The patient still has to pay for the drug or secure funding via another route.

**Funding for a 29c-imported drug** can come from:
- Patient out-of-pocket (often expensive)
- HMO **exceptions committee (ועדת חריגים)** — case-by-case decision
- **Supplementary insurance** (Mushlam Platinum, Maccabi Sheli, Si, Gold) if the plan covers unregistered indications
- Private insurance
- NGO / charity (ICA, Patients' Rights Association, pediatric-cancer NGOs)

**Source**: Kolzchut "Guide to obtaining drugs not in the health basket" — https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%9E%D7%93%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9A_%D7%9C%D7%94%D7%A9%D7%92%D7%AA_%D7%AA%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%A4%D7%95%D7%AA_%D7%A9%D7%90%D7%99%D7%A0%D7%9F_%D7%91%D7%A1%D7%9C_%D7%94%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%90%D7%95%D7%AA

## 2. Compassionate use (טיפול חמלה)

**What it is**: a pharmaceutical company supplies an unregistered or still-experimental drug **free of charge** to an individual patient with a serious or life-threatening illness, under MOH pre-approval.

**Three-party process**:
1. Treating physician identifies a candidate patient
2. Physician approaches the pharmaceutical company to request sponsorship
3. If the company agrees, physician files the request with the **MOH Pharmacy Division** for pre-approval

**For what cases**: serious or life-threatening disease (advanced cancer, ALS, muscular dystrophy, others), no registered Israeli treatment, patient ineligible for or unsuitable for a clinical trial, MOH-assessed benefit > risk.

**Cost**: drug is supplied free of charge by the pharma company when granted.

**CRITICAL — not a right**: "לא קיימת חובה לספק" — there is no obligation on the company to provide the drug, and many companies refuse. Success depends entirely on company willingness.

**Helsinki committee role**: NOT the core pathway for compassionate use. Helsinki = clinical trials. Compassionate use is individual, outside a trial protocol. Helsinki may be involved only when the MOH routes a specific case through institutional ethics review.

**Source**: Kolzchut guide above; Hebrew Wikipedia "טיפול חמלה" — https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D7%98%D7%99%D7%A4%D7%95%D7%9C_%D7%97%D7%9E%D7%9C%D7%94

## 3. Off-label use

**What it is**: a drug that IS registered in Israel for one indication, prescribed for a different indication (a different cancer type, different stage, different combination).

**Legality**: legal in Israel. Physicians may prescribe off-label using clinical judgment. Informed consent (הסכמה מדעת) is standard practice.

**Public basket funding**: the basket generally does NOT fund off-label use. A basket drug is funded only for the specific basket indication.

**Exceptions committee for off-label**: the main public route for off-label funding is the HMO **ועדת חריגים** (Section 5a of the main skill). The patient must demonstrate medical justification + exhaustion of basket alternatives.

**Supplementary insurance off-label clauses (2025)**:

| HMO | Plan | Off-label / unregistered-indication coverage |
|-----|------|--------------------------------------------|
| Clalit | Mushlam Gold / Platinum | Drugs or unregistered indications registered in Israel (or in registration pipeline) with proven efficacy, package cost > ₪356.98. Patient 50% co-pay capped at ₪358/pack/month, total monthly cap ~₪713.97. |
| Maccabi | Maccabi Zahav | Drugs not in the basket or in the basket for a different indication for serious diseases, prior approval required. **24-month waiting period** for items not on the Gold discount list. |
| Meuhedet | Meuhedet Adif / Si | Similar clauses. Verify the 2026 regulations PDF for the patient's specific tier. |
| Leumit | Leumit Kesef / Zahav | Similar clauses. Verify the 2026 regulations PDF. |

**The 2008 reform boundary** (often missed): Israeli supplementary insurance plans have been prohibited since 2008 from covering **life-saving or life-extending drugs as their primary benefit**. The "expanded drug" clauses cover off-label, unregistered-indication, and unregistered-in-Israel scenarios within defined caps. They do NOT replace basket funding for life-saving cancer treatments.

## 4. Clinical trials (expanded)

**Legal basis**: תקנות בריאות העם (ניסויים רפואיים בבני אדם), התשמ"א-1980.

**Helsinki committee (ועדת הלסינקי)** — ethics committee for **research**. Not the same as the HMO funding exceptions committee. Two tiers:

- **Institutional Helsinki committee (ועדת הלסינקי מוסדית)**: at every major hospital. Approves most trials.
- **National Supreme Helsinki Committee (ועדת הלסינקי העליונה)**: handles genetic research and specific elevated-risk categories.

**Where to find oncology trials**:
- **MyTrial** — the MOH official registry, mandatory for trial registration since 2016. Reach it via the Ministry of Health landing page: https://www.gov.il/he/departments/general/clinical-trials-website
- **ClinicalTrials.gov** filtered to Israel — cross-check.

**Who pays during a trial** (general principle, confirm per trial):
- Trial sponsor (typically pharma) pays for the investigational drug and trial-specific procedures
- HMO continues to fund standard-of-care procedures the patient would otherwise receive

**Post-trial access to the drug** (המשך מתן מוצר המחקר לאחר סיום הניסוי): MOH procedure 14 chapter 4 includes a section on this. **Not an automatic patient right.** Sponsor may continue supplying responders subject to Helsinki + MOH agreement. **Discuss this with the principal investigator BEFORE signing the consent form**, not after.

**Source**: MOH clinical-trials procedure (procedure 14) — https://www.gov.il/BlobFolder/policy/cth-14/he/files_publications_units_pharmaceutical_division_clinical_trials_cth-14.pdf

## 5. Named Patient Program (NPP) — does not exist as a separate Israeli category

International patients and some private insurers use the term "Named Patient Program". **Israel has no such named regulatory category.** The two pathways that map to NPP-style access are:

- **Form 29c (תקנה 29)** for individual import
- **טיפול חמלה** for sponsor-provided experimental drugs

If a foreign physician or document refers to "NPP in Israel", clarify which of the two they mean.

## Decision tree: which route for which situation

| Situation | First pathway | Backup |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Drug is in 2026 basket for my indication | Standard prescription via HMO | — |
| Drug registered in Israel for my indication, HMO refused | Exceptions committee | Supplementary if enrolled |
| Drug registered in Israel, off-label for my case | Supplementary off-label clause | Exceptions committee |
| Drug unregistered in Israel, registered abroad | Form 29c by physician + funding via supplementary or exceptions | Private pay or NGO |
| Drug experimental (Phase 2/3, unregistered anywhere) | טיפול חמלה — physician finds pharma sponsor | Clinical trial enrollment if available |
| There is a Phase 3 trial I might qualify for | MyTrial search + oncologist referral + Helsinki-approved enrollment | — |

## Common confusions (patient and agent)

1. **"Form 29c approved → HMO will pay."** WRONG. 29c is an import permit. Funding is separate.
2. **"Compassionate use is a right."** WRONG. The pharma company can refuse, and many do.
3. **"Helsinki committee = exceptions committee."** WRONG. Helsinki = research ethics. Exceptions = HMO funding.
4. **"Supplementary insurance covers life-saving cancer drugs."** WRONG since the 2008 reform. Supplementary covers off-label and unregistered-indication scenarios within caps.
5. **"I'll keep the trial drug forever after the trial."** Not automatic. Negotiated per protocol with Helsinki + MOH.
6. **"There is a Named Patient Program in Israel."** No formal Israeli NPP category. The local terms are 29c and טיפול חמלה.
