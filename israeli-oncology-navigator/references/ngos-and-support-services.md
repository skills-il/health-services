# Verified Israeli Cancer NGOs and Support Services

> Disclaimer: Only NGOs verified against their official website, cancer.org.il, or Kolzchut are listed. Do not extend this list with organizations you cannot verify.

## 1. Israeli Cancer Association (האגודה למלחמה בסרטן / ICA)

**URL:** https://www.cancer.org.il/

The main all-ages, all-cancer-types national organization.

### Telemeda information hotline (free, 24/7, trained volunteers)

| Language | Phone |
|----------|-------|
| Hebrew | 1-800-599-995 |
| Arabic | 1-800-36-36-55 |
| Russian | 1-800-34-33-44 |

### Services

- **Transportation** to and from hospitals, with trained volunteers
- **Wig fitting and loans** at Beit Mati (Givatayim HQ) and ~60 branches
- **Cosmetics and makeup volunteers** who visit oncology wards
- **Accommodation** for out-of-town patients near treating hospitals
- **Rights-realization helpdesk** — phone: **03-5721678**, email: **shikum@cancer.org.il**
- Medical and psycho-social information
- Cancer research funding

Source: ICA hotlines page, ICA contact page, Kolzchut "Information and Support Hotlines for Cancer Patients".

## 2. Larger Than Life (גדולים מהחיים)

**URL:** https://gdolim.org.il/

**Audience:** Children with cancer and their families. A nonprofit founded by parents of children with cancer.

**Name correction:** The correct Hebrew name is **גדולים מהחיים** (Gdolim MeHaChaim). Some internal notes or older references incorrectly write "לרגיש שוב" — this is **not** the correct name. Always use "גדולים מהחיים".

**Focus:** Long-term support and activities for children diagnosed with cancer and their families (pediatric only, not adult).

## 3. Zichron Menachem (זכרון מנחם)

**URL:** https://zichron.org/

**Audience:** Children and young adults aged **0 to 25** with cancer, and their families.

**Services:**
- Retreats
- Activities
- Parental support
- Emotional support
- Hair donation program (for wigs)

Source: zichron.org About page; patients-rights.org organization listing.

## 4. Ezer Mizion (עזר מציון)

**URL:** https://ezermizion.org/

**Services relevant to oncology:**

- **Bone marrow donor registry** — a major Jewish bone marrow donor registry, augmented by routine typing of IDF recruits.
- **Oranit House** — residential accommodation for cancer patients and their families during treatment. Free of charge.
- Broader services for the sick, disabled, and elderly.
- Large volunteer base.

All services are free of charge.

Source: Ezer Mizion bone marrow registry overview page.

## 5. One in Nine (אחת מתשע)

**URL:** https://www.onein9.org.il/

**Focus:** Breast cancer.

Supports women diagnosed with breast cancer and their "support circle" (family members, friends), including specific resources on workplace rights.

## 6. Patients' Rights Association (האגודה לזכויות החולה)

**URL:** https://www.patients-rights.org/

General patient rights across all conditions. Relevant to oncology primarily for:

- Representation in exceptions-committee disputes
- General patient rights advocacy
- Legal aid referral

## 7. Disease-specific organizations (smaller, focused)

- **Israeli Melanoma Association (העמותה הישראלית לסרטן העור)** — https://www.melanoma.org.il/
- **Stop Cancer** — https://www.stop-cancer.co.il/

These are smaller organizations with more specific focus. Verify current activity before referring a patient — smaller NGOs in Israel occasionally go dormant.

## Quick-routing guide

| Patient situation | Primary referral |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Any adult cancer patient, general help | ICA (Telemeda + rights helpdesk) |
| Child diagnosed with cancer, family support | Larger Than Life + Zichron Menachem |
| Young adult (15-25) with cancer | Zichron Menachem |
| Needs bone marrow transplant | Ezer Mizion registry |
| Out-of-town treatment, needs accommodation | Ezer Mizion Oranit House OR ICA accommodation program |
| Breast cancer specifically | One in Nine + ICA |
| HMO refused a drug, needs representation | Patients' Rights Association |
| Needs transportation to treatment | ICA transportation program |

## Important notes to agents

1. **Always tell the user to call the ICA Telemeda hotline.** It is free, 24/7, trilingual, and staffed by trained volunteers.

2. **Do NOT invent organizations.** If you are unsure whether an organization exists or serves Israeli cancer patients, do not mention it. The list above is exhaustive for first-line referrals; anything beyond this list must be verified before naming.

3. **Route children to pediatric NGOs, not general NGOs.** Larger Than Life and Zichron Menachem are the two pediatric-specific choices. The ICA also serves pediatric patients, but the pediatric NGOs provide deeper long-term family support.

4. **All these organizations coordinate with hospital oncology social workers.** The social worker will make the right referral based on diagnosis and family situation.
