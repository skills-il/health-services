# Health Basket (Sal HaBriut) and HMO Supplementary Insurance for Cancer — 2026

> Disclaimer: Figures and plan names are January 2026 values. Supplementary insurance regulations are updated every one or two years; the specific plan's current regulations PDF is the authoritative document. Do not quote a specific non-basket cap without opening the regulations PDF for that plan.

## 1. The 2026 health basket (at a glance)

- **Total new additions:** 107 drugs and technologies
- **Total budget:** ₪650 million (covering roughly 86,450 patients)
- **Oncology share:** approximately ₪337 million (roughly 52% of total)

**Notable 2026 oncology additions:**

- **Imfinzi** — bladder cancer and gastric cancer (peri-operative setting)
- **Tagrisso** — post-operative EGFR-positive non-small-cell lung cancer
- **Keytruda** — head-and-neck cancer; advanced or recurrent endometrial cancer
- **Enhertu** — HER2-low breast cancer
- **Adcetris** — first-line Hodgkin lymphoma

**Before stating that a specific drug is "in the basket":**

1. Open the official 2026 list of medicines PDF (linked at the bottom of this file).
2. Search by generic name (not trade name).
3. Verify the indication, dose, and co-pay match the patient's case.

Drugs in the basket are dispensed by the HMO at the standard drug co-pay. No separate application is required.

Sources: Ministry of Health basket committee 2026 announcement; Pharma Doctors Only 2026 summary; Calcalist 2026 basket coverage.

## 2. HMO Exceptions Committee (ועדת חריגים)

For drugs and treatments **not** in the basket, the mechanism of last resort is the HMO's exceptions committee.

**Trigger:** Formal **written refusal** by the HMO to fund the treatment.

**Required package:**
- Committee-review request form (obtained from the HMO)
- Detailed recommendation from the treating physician, with supporting medical literature and evidence that basket alternatives were tried and exhausted
- All relevant medical records (imaging, pathology, treatment history)

**Committee members:** Physicians (including oncologists), a pharmacologist, a legal advisor, an economic advisor.

**Response timeline:** There is **no statutory deadline**. The Knesset Research Center has flagged timeline transparency as an ongoing concern.

**Representation for patients:** The Patients' Rights Association (https://www.patients-rights.org/) represents patients in exceptions-committee cases.

Source: Kolzchut, "Appealing to the Health Plan Exceptions Committee"; Knesset MMM research paper on exceptions committees.

## 3. HMO supplementary insurance tiers (2026)

Each of the four HMOs sells two supplementary tiers. Non-basket oncology drug coverage is generally only in the **top tier**. Second-opinion coverage is also supplementary-only.

| HMO | Basic supplementary | Top supplementary | Non-basket oncology drug coverage |
|-----|---------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Clalit | Mushlam Zahav | **Mushlam Platinum** | Up to ₪1M cap, including drugs unregistered in Israel if registered in a recognized Western country for the indication |
| Maccabi | Maccabi Zahav | **Maccabi Sheli** | Up to ₪1M cap, drug must be registered in Israel, US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, or EU |
| Meuhedet | Meuhedet Adif | **Meuhedet Si (שיא)** | Non-basket drug discounts on Adif; broader coverage on Si. Verify specific 2026 oncology caps in the plan's regulations PDF |
| Leumit | Leumit Kesef (Silver) | **Leumit Zahav (Gold)** | Life-extending oncology drug coverage historically offered. Verify the 2026 regulations PDF |

**Important caveats:**

- Caps and exclusions vary by plan tier and by renewal date. Always cite the dated regulations PDF for the specific plan.
- For Clalit Mushlam Zahav/Platinum, the standard "extended drug coverage at 50% co-pay up to ₪358 per pack per month" benefit **does NOT apply to oncology/hemato-oncology** — these are excluded from that track and handled under the separate non-basket cap.
- Off-label use coverage is plan-dependent.

## 4. Second Opinion (חוות דעת שנייה)

- **NOT a codified right in the basic basket.** Do not tell a patient on basic-basket-only that they have a "right to a second opinion."
- Delivered through supplementary plans: Mushlam Platinum, Maccabi Sheli, Meuhedet Si, and Leumit Gold all include second-opinion benefits, each with different rules (domestic only vs. domestic plus international, number of opinions covered, physician selection rules).
- Leumit Gold explicitly advertises "second medical opinions" on its plan page.

## 5. Hospice and Palliative Care

Free under the basic basket since **2009**, for patients with a life-threatening illness in the terminal stage (treating physician's prognosis: 6 months or less).

- **Provision:** 24/7 supportive palliative care at home or as an inpatient
- **Team:** physician, nurse, social worker
- **Scope:** cancer AND non-cancer terminal illnesses (end-stage heart, lung, kidney disease, dementia)
- **Access:** family-doctor referral to the HMO home-treatment unit (or the hospital HMO representative if the patient is hospitalized)

Source: wikirefua, "Palliative Service (Hospice) Director General Circular"; Clalit "Home Hospice" page; Kolzchut "Supportive Home Care (Palliative Care)".

## 6. Fertility Preservation Before Cancer Treatment

**Basket coverage** for women facing gonadotoxic therapy (chemotherapy, pelvic radiation):

- Egg freezing, embryo freezing, or ovarian tissue freezing
- Up to age 41 (birthday 42) OR until the woman has 2 children, whichever comes first
- Egg-freezing treatments capped at **4 cycles or 20 eggs total**

**Sperm freezing** for men facing the same treatments is widely covered under the same principle. Verify specific caps with the treating oncology team and the HMO fertility unit.

**This is time-critical.** Must start before cancer treatment begins.

Source: gov.il, "Oocyte cryopreservation"; Kolzchut, "Fertility Preservation"; individual HMO fertility-preservation pages.

## 7. Treatment Abroad Funded by the Basket

Covered when both conditions are met:

1. The insured cannot receive the treatment or a reasonable alternative in Israel
2. The treatment is **life-saving**

Permissible fields explicitly include: congenital defects, organ transplants, **tumors**, cardiovascular disease, CNS disease.

When approved, the HMO funds: the medical treatment, flights, accommodation, food, and **one escort**.

**Process:**
1. Application to the HMO
2. If denied, appeal to the HMO's internal appeals committee (ועדת ערר)

Supplementary plans (Mushlam Platinum, Sheli, Si, Gold) offer parallel, often more generous coverage. Verify the specific plan's regulations.

## 8. Proton Therapy in Israel (2026 status)

**No proton-therapy center is operational in Israel as of 2026.**

- Ichilov (Tel Aviv Sourasky) won the Ministry of Health tender in July 2020
- Purchase agreement for two proton accelerators signed in January 2023
- Expected opening around 2026 (verify current status before stating the center is open or not)

Patients who need proton therapy today are funded abroad through the basket treatment-abroad track (section 7 above).
